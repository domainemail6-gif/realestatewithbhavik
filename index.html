Something worth knowing

Every project has a challenge the seller won't mention.

In my experience, for most projects in the NCR market, under-construction and ready-to-move both, there is at least one meaningful problem that the broker or the developer's sales team will quietly work around. It might be a pending RERA compliance, a delivery track record that doesn't hold up to scrutiny, a carpet-to-super ratio that makes the per-square-foot math uncomfortable, or infrastructure around the project that's five years away on an optimistic timeline.

This isn't a sweeping indictment of every builder. Some projects are genuinely good. But the incentive to hide the uncomfortable part is always there, because disclosure slows down the sale.

Part of what I do is find that uncomfortable part before you sign, not after. If it's a dealbreaker, you should know that. If it's manageable, you should be making a decision with that priced in.
